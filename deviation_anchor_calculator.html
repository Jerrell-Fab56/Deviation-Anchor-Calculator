Rope slides freely through deviation carabiner/pulley — tension is equal on both sides. Drop = vertical distance from main anchor to deviation anchor point.
Deviation force F = T × sin(θ) where θ = angle from vertical, T = load weight.
Deviations are low-force anchors — at 20° the deviation sees only ~34% of the load.
Limits per SPRAT/industry practice: deflection ≤ 20° from vertical AND horizontal offset ≤ 6 ft.
Does not account for rope friction through carabiner. Static load only.
All life safety systems must be approved by qualified personnel per applicable standards.
